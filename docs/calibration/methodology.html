Track-Record Methodology

Peer-reviewed statistical foundation behind the public calibration dashboard. Source: github.com/skippALGO/skipp-algo.

← Dashboard
One-paragraph summary. The track record evaluates four structurally independent SMC setup families — Break-of-Structure (BOS), Order-Block (OB), Fair-Value-Gap (FVG), Liquidity-Sweep (SWEEP) — against an event-by-event ledger. Each detected setup emits a calibrated probability; the realised outcome (win/loss, R-multiple, FVG fill) is automatically backfilled. The resulting (predicted_prob, outcome) pairs feed a pipeline of walk-forward validation, bootstrap confidence intervals, permutation tests with Phipson-Smyth correction, Benjamini-Hochberg FDR control across families × metrics, Probabilistic Sharpe Ratio, and a mandatory live-incubation phase of three months minimum. Every method below is peer-reviewed and cited. We invented none of them. The combination — and the public, automated, cron-published full history with no cherry-picking — is the differentiator.

The four setup families

The setup type is a hard four-value enumeration in the codebase (smc_core/scoring.py:33): EventFamily = Literal["BOS", "OB", "FVG", "SWEEP"]. Each detected setup lands in exactly one family. Aggregation is strictly per family — mixing would superimpose probability distributions and invalidate the statistics.

FamilySmart-Money conceptOutcome definition
BOSBreak of Structure (higher high / lower low)Follow-through holds the break across the holding period
OBOrder Block (last high-volume zone before break)Price retests and respects the zone
FVGFair Value Gap (unfilled price imbalance)Gap fills within holding period (FVG fill)
SWEEPLiquidity Sweep (stop-hunt past key level)Price reverses after sweep, closes beyond sweep level

How a trade enters the track record

1 · Event ledger entry on detection

When the SMC core detects a setup, it appends a row to the event ledger (smc_core/event_ledger.py:37–54):

{"family": "FVG", "predicted_prob": 0.65, "outcome": null, "features": {...}, "outcome_extras": {...}, "timestamp": "..."}

predicted_prob is the post-calibration probability — it has already passed the Platt-scaling layer.

2 · Outcome backfill

After 1d / 3d / 5d forward-holding period, outcome is automatically backfilled. R-multiple and win/loss flag are added in outcome_extras. Pipeline: open-prep-outcome-backfill.yml.

3 · Daily aggregation per family

emit_public_calibration_report.py aggregates to one row per day in calibration_report_public.json and appends to calibration_report_public_history.jsonl.

4 · Cron publication

public-calibration-dashboard.yml runs weekdays at 04:30 UTC and commits updates to the repo. Output is publicly readable, SHA-verifiable, and cannot be silently rewritten.

Statistical hardening, step by step

Brier score and ECE — calibration metrics

Brier scoreMean squared error (p − outcome)² across eventsYes

Hit rateShare of correct directional callsNo (higher is better)

Walk-forward validation

Strictly rolling windows: train calibrator on months 1–6, test on month 7, slide forward by one month, repeat. No re-optimisation on out-of-sample results. Walk-forward efficiency (WFE) must exceed 50%.

Bootstrap confidence intervals

Per-trade resampling with 2000 iterations for Sharpe, profit factor, win rate, max drawdown. BCa intervals preferred over naive percentile. The lower 95% CI of Sharpe must exceed 0.3.

Permutation test with Phipson-Smyth correction

Brier and ECE have no closed-form null distribution. We use the permutation test:

  1. Pool all events from treatment and control arms
  2. Randomly partition into the original sizes
  3. Compute Brier difference under this synthetic world
  4. Repeat 2000 times (BOOTSTRAP_B = 2000, BOOTSTRAP_SEED = 42)
  5. Compute the p-value with the Phipson-Smyth formula:
p = (r + 1) / (B + 1)

The +1 correction prevents p = 0 — necessary because downstream log-transforms in combined-evidence layers would otherwise blow up.

Benjamini-Hochberg FDR correction

With 4 families × 3 metrics = 12 simultaneous tests, the naive false-positive probability under H₀ without correction is:

1 − (1 − 0.05)^12 ≈ 0.46

That is: under a null treatment, almost every other run would falsely flag "significant". BH-FDR sorts the 12 raw p-values ascending and controls the expected false-discovery rate at q = 0.05. We correct jointly across all 12 cells, not per metric — separate corrections would inflate effective FDR to ~0.15 (Simes-Bonferroni argument).

Regime stratification

Market-regime tagger (Bull / Bear / Range / Vola-High / Vola-Low) based on SPY returns. Per setup × regime: separate win rate, Sharpe, n. Edges must hold in more than one regime, otherwise the family is downgraded to "incubation".

Probabilistic Sharpe Ratio + MinTRL

Adjusts the observed Sharpe for skewness and kurtosis of returns. PSR(SR*=0) must exceed 0.95. MinTRL states explicitly how many additional trades are needed to prove SR > threshold — preventing premature promotion.

Live incubation

Even after a perfect backtest, a strategy is sellable only after 3–6 months of live trading without re-optimisation. Phase A (4 weeks paper trading at 100% size), Phase B (3–6 months live at 10–25% size). The live-Sharpe ÷ backtest-Sharpe ratio must be ≥ 0.50.

Known limits and accepted residual risk

Reproducibility

  • Determinism: BOOTSTRAP_SEED = 42 hard-coded. Re-run with the same seed reproduces identical output bit-for-bit.
  • Pure-Python stdlib: no NumPy/SciPy hard dependency for the statistics layer.
  • SHA verification: every dashboard commit embeds commit_sha in the report; every outcome entry is git-tagged.
  • Open source: the full statistics layer is readable in the repo — no black-box model, no proprietary algorithm.
  • Audit trail: the history file is append-only — no rewrite of past entries.

Disclaimer. Backtest results and live incubation are not a guarantee of future returns. Markets can transition into regimes where historical edges do not hold. The track record is intended as a methodologically robust benchmark, not as a promise — the honest "inactive" labelling of failing families is part of that discipline.
